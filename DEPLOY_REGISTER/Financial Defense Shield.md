## 1. Introduction

The *Financial Defense Shield* is an original figurative trademark designed to represent a modern, global system of protection for consumers exposed to financial fraud.  
The emblem combines:

- a contemporary protective shield,  
- an asymmetric upper profile inspired by ancient legionary shields,  
- concentric vigilance rings in yellow,  
- and the distinctive signature **M • © • S**.

This visual identity symbolizes vigilance, integrity, and the defense of individuals against increasingly sophisticated financial crimes.

## 2. Blockchain-Based Proof of Authorship

To ensure immutable authorship and priority, the trademark has been minted as three Non‑Fungible Tokens (NFTs) on public, decentralized, Ethereum‑compatible blockchains:

- **Polygon PoS**  
- **Base (Ethereum Layer 2)**  
- **Celo Network**

All records are **publicly verifiable on‑chain**, providing independent, tamper‑proof evidence of creation.

The three NFTs contain **identical metadata to ensure consistency of evidence**, including:

- the original high‑resolution artwork of the figurative trademark,  
- descriptive metadata,  
- and a cryptographic hash that guarantees the artwork cannot be altered without invalidating the blockchain record.

This multi‑chain approach strengthens the evidentiary value for international intellectual property protection.

## 4. Psychological Self‑Defense and Consumer Protection

The project introduces a pioneering concept:  
**psychological self‑defense as the first barrier against financial fraud**.

The webpages provide:

- self‑assessment tools for emotional vulnerability,  
- educational content in ten languages,  
- and guidance for recognizing manipulation techniques used by fraudsters.

While banks rely solely on MIFID questionnaires—focused on financial literacy—these tools address the *emotional vulnerability* that precedes financial victimization.

This approach fills a critical gap in global consumer protection.

## 5. European Petitions and Institutional Impact

Attorney Marcello Stanca is the author of two European Parliament Petitions:

- **Petition 0888/2024**  
- **Petition 0645/2025**

Both were formally commended and accepted by the European Parliament.

These initiatives contributed to the **Agreement of 27 November 2025** between the Economic‑Financial Committee and the European Parliament, establishing the principle of **strict liability** for:

- banks,  
- payment service providers (PSPs),  
- and major web platforms

that fail to block fraudulent content or dangerous websites.

The *Financial Defense Shield* ecosystem disseminates these documents in ten languages, promoting global awareness and harmonization of anti‑fraud measures.
