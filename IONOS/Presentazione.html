Financial Defense Shield

Institutional communication on EU Petitions and global anti‑fraud platforms

Dear Presidents, Directors and Officers of the Financial Conduct Authorities,

I wish to inform you of two legislative initiatives that I submitted to the European Parliament, both of which have been formally accepted and incorporated into the political agreement reached on 27 November 2025 between the European Parliament and the Council of the EU, within the new regulatory framework for payment services (PSR and PSD3).

1. Accepted EU Petitions (0888/2024 and 0645/2025)

In 2024 and 2025, on behalf of the AMEV Firenze Association, I submitted the following Petitions:

  • Petition No. 0888/2024 – On the obligation for banks to protect clients from fake online investments.
  • Petition No. 0645/2025 – On measures to counter financial fraud committed through bank transfers and credit cards.

Both Petitions were accepted and contributed to the introduction of key principles in the 27 November 2025 agreement, including:

  • direct liability of banks and payment service providers for failure to prevent fraud;
  • mandatory verification of the match between beneficiary name and unique identifier;
  • automatic blocking of suspicious transactions and immediate reimbursement of identity‑theft victims;
  • liability of online platforms that fail to remove fraudulent content;
  • mandatory proof of legal authorization for financial advertisers;
  • greater transparency of fees and enhanced consumer protection.

2. International Platforms: tutelatruffe.it and scam‑radar.com

Alongside this institutional work, I developed two digital platforms dedicated to the prevention of online financial fraud:

  • tutelatruffe.it – Italian portal for the protection of users against online scams, fake brokers and unauthorized platforms.
  • scam‑radar.com – International version designed for global users and for cooperation with supervisory authorities.

Both platforms include:

  • a 24/7 continuous‑analysis engine (“software‑legionary”);
  • a traffic‑light risk system (green – yellow – red);
  • a monitoring tool for suspicious websites and fraudulent platforms;
  • translation buttons in 10 languages for global accessibility.

These platforms are designed as tools to support prevention, reporting and cooperation between citizens, institutions and supervisory authorities worldwide.
